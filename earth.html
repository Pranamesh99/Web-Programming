Earth

Introduction

Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only known planet to support life. It is often referred to as the "Blue Planet" due to its vast oceans that cover 71% of its surface.

Key Facts

  • Diameter: 12,742 km
  • Orbital Period: 365.25 days
  • Rotation Period: 24 hours
  • Distance from the Sun: About 149.6 million km
  • Moons: 1 (The Moon)

Surface and Atmosphere

Earth's surface is incredibly diverse, ranging from vast oceans and towering mountains to expansive deserts and dense forests. Its atmosphere is composed primarily of nitrogen (78%) and oxygen (21%), which supports a wide variety of life forms.

Life on Earth

Earth is home to millions of species, including humans. Its unique conditions, such as liquid water, a stable climate, and a protective atmosphere, make it a cradle for life.

Exploration

Although Earth is our home, it has been extensively studied through satellites, telescopes, and scientific research. These efforts have provided insights into its history, climate, and dynamic processes.
