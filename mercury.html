Mercury

Introduction

Mercury is the closest planet to the Sun and the smallest planet in the Solar System. Despite its small size, it plays a significant role in our understanding of planetary formation and evolution.

Key Facts

  • Diameter: 4,880 km
  • Orbital Period: 88 Earth days
  • Rotation Period: 59 Earth days
  • Distance from the Sun: 57.91 million km
  • Moons: None

Surface and Atmosphere

Mercury has a rocky surface covered with craters, resembling the Moon. Its extremely thin atmosphere, known as an exosphere, is composed primarily of oxygen, sodium, hydrogen, helium, and potassium. The lack of a substantial atmosphere results in extreme temperature variations.

Exploration

Two spacecraft have visited Mercury:

  • Mariner 10: Conducted flybys in the 1970s, capturing the first close-up images of Mercury.
  • MESSENGER: Orbited Mercury from 2011 to 2015, providing detailed data on its surface, composition, and magnetic field.
