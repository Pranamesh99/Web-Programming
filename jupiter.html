Jupiter

Introduction

Jupiter is the fifth planet from the Sun and the largest planet in the Solar System. Its immense size, powerful magnetic field, and iconic Great Red Spot make it a fascinating object of study.

Key Facts

  • Diameter: 139,820 km
  • Orbital Period: 11.86 Earth years
  • Rotation Period: About 9.9 hours
  • Distance from the Sun: About 778.5 million km
  • Moons: 79 (including the four largest: Io, Europa, Ganymede, and Callisto)

Atmosphere and Composition

Jupiter is a gas giant, primarily composed of hydrogen and helium, with traces of ammonia, methane, and water vapor. The Great Red Spot, a massive storm persisting for centuries, highlights its turbulent atmosphere.

Exploration

Several spacecraft have explored Jupiter:

  • Pioneer Missions: The first close encounters with Jupiter in the 1970s.
  • Voyager Missions: Provided detailed images and data about Jupiter's atmosphere and moons.
  • Galileo: Orbited Jupiter from 1995 to 2003, studying its system extensively.
  • Juno: Currently studying Jupiter's atmosphere, magnetosphere, and interior since 2016.
