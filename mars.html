Mars

Introduction

Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun and is often referred to as the "Red Planet" due to its reddish appearance, caused by iron oxide (rust) on its surface. It has captured human imagination for centuries and remains a primary target for space exploration.

Key Facts

  • Diameter: 6,779 km
  • Orbital Period: 687 Earth days
  • Rotation Period: 24.6 hours
  • Distance from the Sun: About 227.9 million km
  • Moons: 2 (Phobos and Deimos)

Surface and Atmosphere

Mars has a thin atmosphere composed mostly of carbon dioxide, with traces of nitrogen and argon. Its surface is marked by:

  • Olympus Mons: The largest volcano in the Solar System, standing about 21.9 km tall.
  • Valles Marineris: A canyon system over 4,000 km long and up to 7 km deep.
  • Polar Ice Caps: Composed of water and carbon dioxide ice, these caps grow and recede with the seasons.

Exploration

Mars has been explored extensively through orbiters, landers, and rovers. Notable missions include:

  • Spirit and Opportunity: Twin rovers that provided valuable geological insights.
  • Curiosity: A rover studying the planet's climate and geology since 2012.
  • Perseverance: A rover searching for signs of ancient life and collecting samples for a future return to Earth.
